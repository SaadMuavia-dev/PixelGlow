Before You Ask

Frequently Asked Questions

Here are the questions we receive most often. If yours isn't here, just ask — we're happy to answer anything.

How far in advance should I get in touch?
As early as possible — ideally 2–3 months before your deadline. Our current lead time is 4–6 weeks, but popular windows fill quickly. Rush projects are occasionally possible at a premium; please enquire directly.
Do you work with self-publishing authors?
Absolutely. A significant portion of our work is with independent and self-publishing authors. We provide print-ready files and KDP-compatible formatting, along with clickable tables of contents for e-books, ready for KDP, IngramSpark, and other major POD platforms.
How many revisions are included?
All projects include one round of revisions in the final layout stage. The early structure and concept stages have unlimited feedback rounds — we'd rather get the structure right at the beginning than make changes at the end.
What file formats will I receive?
You'll receive everything you need for your use case — print-ready PDFs (CMYK, 300dpi, bleed included), editable layout files, and high-resolution graphics. EPUB and KDP-ready files are available for digital book and self-publishing projects.
Do you retain copyright of the design work?
You receive a licence to use the final layout and design as agreed in the brief (e.g. for the book in all editions). We retain the right to display the work in our portfolio. Full copyright transfer is available for an additional fee — please discuss at brief stage.
What is your payment structure?
A 50% deposit is required to secure your project slot, with the balance due on delivery of final files. For larger projects we offer milestone-based payment schedules. We accept bank transfer and most major payment platforms.
Can you work to a very tight deadline?
Sometimes — it depends on our current schedule. Rush projects (turnaround under 2 weeks) carry a 30% premium. Please always reach out even if your deadline feels impossibly tight; we'll tell you honestly whether we can help.

Still have questions? Just ask.

No question is too small. We'd rather answer ten questions upfront than have any ambiguity in the brief.
